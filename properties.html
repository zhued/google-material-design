Material design has concise, immutable characteristics and inherent behaviors that add to the relationship between an object and the enviornment that it lives in along with actions it can perform.

Physical

Objects can vary in both the x and y axis, but every object MUST be of 1 dp (pixel) thickness. It is very unnatural to the human eye to see objects be 3D on a 2D screen; it automatically doesn't seem as "real" or engaging when there is random thicknesses.

NO

Content will NEVER add thickness to it's current plane, therefore information must live within the boundries of each layer or platform. This makes it very easy for users to distinguish between what content is being presented, and there will be no "bleeding" through of other information to cause confusion.

Multiple elements must not occupy the same point in space simultaneously. This would break the flow of focus from within the page as the user will not be able to distinguish between what information should be correlated together.

YES
NO

Material is solid. So elements CANNOT simply pass through other elements; that would absolute magic if it were to happen in the user's physical world and it would make the transition difficult to understand for some users.

NO

Transforming

Although objects cannot pass through each other or be on the same plane, objects can transform and change it's purpose or data. This is a visual effect that everyone understands: something becomes something else. Simple. And if done with clarity, it will bring an simple but effect way of transitioning that all users will understand.

Folding is an interesting topic. Often times when we flip or fold a peice of paper, we are reminded of magic tricks or origami; two concepts of expertise and forms of disorientation. Although flipping and folding looks cool, the animations do not have much meaning to them, and we cannot afford to have an abundance of animations that will only confuse users in the long run.

NO

Material objects can be combined together and also disconnected. Simple for users to understand. Content becomes relevant? Connect. Content becomes unrelevant? Disconnect.

And also reorganized.

Movement

Material elements can be created or destroyed spontaneously. As simple to understand as a zoom in and zoom out.

Material elements can also move across any axis, allowing for smooth, fluid motions for the user to see exactly where content is going.
